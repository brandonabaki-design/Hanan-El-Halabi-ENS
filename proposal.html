Sections 9 & 10

Three Funding Pathways & an Alternative Cross-Subsidy Option

Three flexible mechanisms to fund specialist provision sustainably — plus an optional universal levy that could reduce reliance on direct ALN charging entirely.

1

ENS Hires / Parents Pay ENS (Annual)

Fully in-house staffing model
  • Applies to Placements A – C
  • Applies to Placement D (where not mandated as Tier 3 statutory provision)
  • Subject to tuition percentage caps — ADEK rule: not exceeding 50% of annual fees
2

ENS Outsources / Parents Pay ENS (Monthly)

Pass-through model with transparent monthly billing
  • ENS pays the external provider directly
  • Parents pay ENS on a 10-month pro-rated basis
  • ENS passes the cost through transparently — no margin added
3

ENS Outsources / Parents Pay Provider Directly

For therapies and highly specialized services
  • Therapies and highly specialized services delivered on-site
  • ENS provides MOUs, learning space, and safeguarding governance
  • ENS may charge up to a 10% management fee on provider services (subject to regulatory authority confirmation)

Section 8

Placement Types — Tiered Specialist Provision

Four placement types (A → D) describe escalating levels of specialist support. The chargeable component reflects only what is delivered beyond standard inclusive practice and minimum regulatory staffing.

A

Regular class with indirect support / resource assistance

Lightest level of additional provision

The chargeable component relates to the formal allocation of additional inclusion coordination and educational oversight beyond the school's standard inclusive practices.

The charge covers

  • Designation of Inclusion Team capacity for individualized monitoring and documentation beyond routine classroom tracking (excluding qualifying K–2 Tier 3 students in Abu Dhabi ENS schools)
  • Additional planning, coordination, and follow-up to support consistent implementation of agreed accommodations
  • Centralized analysis and reporting of progress outcomes related to the student's additional learning needs
  • Scheduled inclusion team involvement in assessment planning and exam-related access arrangements
i
Placement A does not include the delivery of instructional intervention or additional staffing — it reflects the resourcing of individualized inclusion oversight that exceeds baseline classroom support.
B

Regular class with withdrawal assistance

Targeted educational intervention added to mainstream

The chargeable component includes targeted educational intervention delivered in addition to standard inclusive classroom practices.

The charge covers

  • Scheduled and timetabled withdrawal sessions delivered by inclusion specialists, exceeding the capacity of minimum staffing workloads
  • Individual or small-group instructional intervention aligned to identified learning priorities, exceeding minimum staffing capacity
  • Additional specialist time allocated for planning, delivering, and reviewing intervention outcomes, exceeding minimum staffing capacity
  • Enhanced inclusion team involvement to monitor progress and adjust intervention strategies, exceeding minimum staffing capacity
i
Placement B applies where regular instructional support beyond classroom differentiation is required, necessitating additional specialist staffing time beyond minimum school inclusion provision.
C

Special Education class with partial integration

Specialist class for one subject or instructional block

The chargeable component consists of specialist educational provision delivered through partial placement in a special education setting.

The charge covers

  • Access to a Special Education class for one subject or defined instructional block
  • Individualized curriculum and assessment design significantly adapted from age-expected standards
  • Allocation of specialist teaching staff and shared inclusion assistant support, where applicable
  • Planned, supported integration into mainstream lessons in line with student readiness
  • Increased documentation, reporting, and specialist oversight associated with this level of provision
!
Placement C reflects specialist instruction that cannot be delivered through withdrawal support alone and therefore requires dedicated specialist resourcing beyond standard inclusion staffing.
D

Special Education class — full-time

Highest level of school-based specialist provision

The chargeable component relates to full-time access to advanced specialist educational provision.

The charge covers

  • Full-time placement in Special Education classes across multiple subjects
  • Highly individualized curriculum and assessment frameworks aligned to significant learning needs
  • Sustained specialist teacher input and reduced instructional ratios
  • Coordination of specialist resources and staffing required to deliver consistent, intensive support
  • Comprehensive monitoring and reporting of academic, functional, and developmental progress
!
Regulatory exception: where full-time specialist provision is mandated by ADEK (e.g., Tier 3 ALN students in KG–Grade 2), this placement is provided as statutory provision and is NOT chargeable.
i
Important: Charges associated with placement levels apply exclusively to additional and specialist educational provision delivered beyond the school's standard inclusive practices and minimum regulatory staffing requirements. Standard inclusive provision is not subject to additional charges.
Sections 6 & 7

Services Scope — Standard vs. Chargeable Provision

A clear line between what ENS continues to provide free of charge as baseline inclusion (protecting inspection judgements) and what may be charged where provision exceeds the regulatory minimum.

6. Standard Inclusion Services NON-CHARGEABLE

ENS will continue to provide the following at no charge — protecting inspection judgments on inclusion quality:

  • Non-clinical screening and identification
  • Individual Education Plan (IEP) development and review
  • Teacher coaching and parent consultation related to IEPs
  • Staff professional development on identified needs
  • Minimum required push-in and pull-out support staffing
  • Curriculum accommodations and modifications
  • Assessment accommodations and access arrangements
  • Educational resources addressing functional, behavioural, social-emotional, communication, and cognitive needs (non-clinical)

7. Services That May Be Chargeable BEYOND BASELINE

Chargeable services apply only where provision exceeds minimum staffing and regulatory requirements, including:

  • Hiring additional Inclusion Assistants or Inclusion Teachers
  • Personalized curriculum development significantly below grade-level
  • Personalized assessment frameworks significantly below grade-level
  • Specialized vocational or functional pathways
  • Special education classes exceeding minimum entitlement
  • Extended hours or enhanced staffing ratios
i
Guiding principle: Standard inclusive provision, as defined by ADEK and other regulatory authorities, is not subject to additional charges. Charges apply exclusively to additional and specialist educational provision delivered beyond the school's standard inclusive practices and minimum regulatory staffing requirements.

3.1 Mandatory Staffing Requirements (Per K–12 School in Abu Dhabi)

The following minimum staffing structure is non-negotiable:

  • 1 Head of Inclusion per school (already met across ENS)
  • 1 Inclusion Teacher per cycle
  • 1 Inclusion Assistant per cycle (push-in / pull-out rotation)
  • 1:1 Inclusion Assistant for every Tier 3 K–2 student
!
Across the three Abu Dhabi schools, this requires 22 one-to-one Inclusion Assistants plus additional cycle-based staffing for coverage and rotation.

What this means in practice

ENS must move from a discretionary inclusion model to a regulated minimum staffing model, with documented compliance, ratios, and dedicated learning spaces. Without action, ENS faces:

  • Non-compliance findings during ADEK inspection
  • Inspection downgrade or stagnation in inclusion judgements
  • Operational and financial strain on existing staffing budgets
Section 1

Executive Summary

A controlled, ethical transition from a fully school-absorbed inclusion model to a hybrid sustainability model — protecting access, equity, compliance, and inspection performance.

Where ENS stands today

Emirates National Schools (ENS) has historically delivered inclusion and Additional Learning Needs (ALN) provision without charging families, absorbing all staffing and resource costs within operational budgets. This model has enabled broad access but is no longer sustainable given:

  • Regulatory tightening across the Emirates
  • Rising identification rates of students with additional needs
  • Mandated minimum staffing ratios per cycle and per school

From AY 2026–2027: new non-negotiable compliance requirements

Particularly in Abu Dhabi, ENS will be required to provide:

  • One-to-one Inclusion Assistants (IAs) for Tier 3 ALN students in Grades K–2
  • Minimum staffing structures by cycle and by school
  • Continued provision of in-house specialist services via ADEK-approved centers
  • Dedicated learning spaces to support pull-out and individualized instruction
